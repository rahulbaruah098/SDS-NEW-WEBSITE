    The Role of Community-Based Organisations

    • Mobilising Indigenous Knowledge: Fishers bring insights on unusual flooding, changes in salinity, and shifting river patterns.
    • Investing in Resilience: CBOs can pool resources for flood-proof ponds, hatcheries, cold storage, biofloc systems, and recirculatory aquaculture systems (RAS), all supported by PMMSY.
    • Delivering Training and Support: They provide hands-on guidance on fish health management, sustainable stocking, feed practices, and disease prevention.
    • Reducing Vulnerability: By running savings groups, disaster funds, and insurance schemes, they enhance financial security for fishers.
    • Market Linkages: FPOs improve bargaining power, ensuring access to inputs and guaranteed markets.

    The Path Forward

    • Scaling climate-smart aquaculture technologies (biofloc, RAS, improved hatcheries).
    • Strengthening cold chain infrastructure and market linkages.
    • Expanding credit access, insurance coverage, and governance support for small fishers.
    • Empowering CBOs and FPOs as central drivers of change.
