    Context & Ecosystem

    Farmer Producer Companies/Organisations (FPC/FPO) or Producer Organisations (PO) are legal entities of primary producers—farmers, milk producers, fishers, weavers, artisans—where members share profits and benefits. Under forms like producer companies, institutions of primary producers can also be members, with a core criterion that shareholders are producers themselves (FPO, NABARD, 2015). The Central Sector Scheme on Formation & Promotion of 10,000 FPOs (₹6,865 crore; 2021) is implemented via IAs such as NCDC, SFAC, NAFED, NABARD, engaging Cluster-Based Business Organisations (CBBOs) to register and handhold each FPO for five years.

    CBBO Role & ODOP

    CBBOs develop FPOs within producer clusters, leverage scale, and improve market access. ODOP clusters enable specialization, processing, branding, and exports, linking at least 60% members' produce.

    Women in Leadership

    Women are shareholders and signatory BoDs. Ujjwal Women FPC’s Marry Narzary (also a Krishi Sakhi) is mobilizing women in Udalguri’s Kobirali to join, train, and grow livelihoods through FPO activity.

    Agri-Business & Inputs

    FPOs trade rice, maize, mustard; sell fertilizers and seeds at minimal margin, easing input costs for farmers while building sustainable turnover.

    Value Addition

    Siroheuji Women FPO processes and sells pickles under its label—generating regular income and reducing members’ burdens of storage, marketing, and customer outreach.

    Capacity & Governance

    CBBO teams train and monitor CEOs and accountants on operations, accounts, cashbooks, and MIS, while sustaining member motivation and facilitating BoD business planning.

    Finance & Policy

    Credit through mainstream institutions, stronger shareholder ownership, and collectivisation of FPCs at state/national level are essential, notes Parag Baruah, MD, SDS.

    Field Voices & Momentum

    “We have a target of one crore business for the upcoming financial year,” says Abida Begum, Director of Darangi Women FPC, as markets in Pub Mangaldai hum with activity. In Deuri gaon, the Swarang Women Producers Co‑operative—amid jujube and buckwheat fields—navigates awareness and trust challenges. Its CEO, Ms. Jayanti Boro, has sought training support from Mr. Saunak Bhuyan, Program Leader, SDS. SDS is empanelled to promote 67 FPOs across Assam, Tripura, Manipur, Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Odisha, and Bihar with IAs including SFAC, NAFED, NCDC, NFDB, and TRLM.

    CEO Dhrubojyoti Saikia of Siroheuji FPO notes that value‑added products like pickles create steady, manageable revenue streams. By June 2024, 8,875 FPOs were registered under the central scheme (PIB), and the recent Union Budget earmarked ₹584 crore for agri‑allied sectors—signalling a strengthening environment for women shareholders and producers.
